  Airport - Anuradhapura

    Anuradhapura can be considered as the ancient capital and the first capital of Sri Lanka. The city of Anuradhapura was ruled by 119 kings and was the financial center of Buddhism in Sri Lanka. This city, located in the North Central Province, can be reached by traveling 205 km from Colombo.

    Famous places in Anuradhapura:

    • Ruwanweliseya
    • Sri Maha Bodhi
    • Isurumuniya Rajamaha Viharaya
    • Abhayagiriya Viharaya
    • Thuparamaya
    • Jetavanaramaya
    • Samadhi Buddha Statue
    • Lovamahapaya
    • Wilpattu National Park
    • Ranmasu Uyana
    • Anuradhapura Moonstone
 Habanara / Sigiriya / Polonnaruwa

    On the 3rd day, we will take you to the Habarana, Sigiriya, and Polonnaruwa areas. Habarana is a popular destination for safari enthusiasts due to its forested areas and the presence of elephants. Next, we will visit Sigiriya, a magnificent mountain steeped in legend and history related to the region's kings, which our guide will explain to you in detail. Finally, we will explore the ancient ruins and Buddhist monuments of Polonnaruwa, which are spread across a wide area. There are numerous places to see, and our guide will take you to all the places that you wish to visit.

    Kandy City.

  Nuwara Eliya

    Afterwards, proceed to Nuwara Eliya.

    Nuwara Eliya is a city located in the Central Province of Sri Lanka, and is one of the most attractive destinations for tourists. Also known as the City of Light, it has a mild climate and offers breathtaking scenery. The city is situated at an altitude of 1,868 meters, and is the center of tea production in Sri Lanka.

  Ella

    Afterwards, proceed to Ella

    This city is governed by a city council and is located in Badulla district. It is an umbrella city situated at a distance of 200 km from Colombo, at an altitude of 1041m above sea level. The city has rich biodiversity and enjoys a cool climate, making it a very beautiful area.

    • Nine Arches Bridge
    • Little Adam's Peak
    • Ravana's Cave
    • Ella Spice Garden Cooking
    • Diyaluma Falls
  Yala National Park

    Yala is the second largest national park in Sri Lanka, located in the southeastern region of the country, spanning across both the Southern and Uva provinces. The sanctuary is abundant with wild animals, including elephants, leopards, and various bird species, and was designated as a sanctuary in 1900.

    • Buduruwagala

 Hikkaduwa.

    Hikkaduwa

    A city located in the Galle district, this beautiful area attracts many foreign and local visitors who come to enjoy coral reefs and various water sports. The city provides great services to the tourism industry and is also equipped to fulfill the business needs of the Sri Lankan people. It is located 98 km from Colombo and is a suitable location for those seeking a private and peaceful life.

    • Turtle Farm
    • Hikkaduwa Beach
    • Seenigama Vihara
    • Moonstone Mines
