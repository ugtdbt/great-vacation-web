  ABOUT RAMAYANA TOUR PACKAGE

    This tour offers an opportunity to explore the origins of the ancient Indian epic, The Ramayana, which conveys the importance of relationships, ideal characters, and the concept of Dharma. Sri Lanka is home to more than 50 Ramayana sites, including the location where Seetha Devi was held captive, battlefields where vast armies fought, groves of exotic herbs dropped by the monkey god Hanuman, and the place where Lord Rama defeated Ravana, the ten-headed demon-king.

    The local people still remember the significance of their soil to the epic, and an oath taken at the spot where Seetha Devi underwent 'Agni Pariksha' remains valid in village courts or grama sabhas. The soil of the ancient battlefield is still red today, surrounded by lighter earth, while the scorched-earth look of one of Ravana's airports, torched by Hanuman, is marked by a patch of darker soil surrounded by brown earth. The legacy of Hanuman's heroic voyage carrying a mountain with life-restoring herbs can be seen in the presence of exotic alpine Himalayan species amidst tropical Sri Lankan vegetation.

    Despite the social, cultural, and religious changes that have taken place in Sri Lanka, the names of places associated with the epic have remained unchanged over time.

They cover everything from leading tourist attractions and restaurants to hotels and how to get to a certain spot.
